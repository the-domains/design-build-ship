How

If you're anything

coffee. In

cup

Usually, I go to the
Starbucks
near my

house because it's



convenient. And

always,

interesting decision.

Deal or no
deal

That's the choice a
potential
customer
is
given when
there's


only a single
buying

option on

the table.





So many companies
do this,
and end up

telling would be



customers to buy
their


product, or
leave.





Can you guess what
usually
happens?

Starbucks
isn't one
of

those



companies.

What size

would you

like?

Let's

look

at

your





options:

  • Tall, which is
    12oz and
    costs
    $2.85





  • Grande, which is
    16oz and
    costs

    $3.45





  • Venti, which is
    20oz and
    costs
    $3.95





Ok, each version of
the
product has a

4oz difference,

increase

equally.
Cool.





But then why isn't

The difference
between the

Grande and

between
the Grande

and

Venti it's only 50¢.





Plus, going from the

Grande is

a whole number
up


($2 to $3), making it

Why would

If I get the

Venti, I'm

more



ounces of

what



seems like

much

This is

decoy
effect

If they just had two

say the Tall

at $2.85 and the



Venti at $3.95, they

would

have a
problem:





In this scenario,
there is no
clearly
better
choice.





Sure, the Venti has
a higher
price tag,

that


extra $1.10 isn't

totally
worth

Leaving it up to the
customer

this question is just



as bad as not giving


them a
choice at all.
Doing this means




that they can't really
come to
an

informed
decision,
which


could lead to

analysis paralysis

(a.k.a. no sale
because

can't

decide which one is

So let's
make it
easy
for
them

By adding that third
Grande
option,

Starbucks has given



potential customers

a

reference point to
compare
costs
with.


But, it's an

unreliable narrator.





It has to be skewed
one way

in this case
making


the Venti the clear



choice. Otherwise it
would just
add to

the

stress of
decision
making,
and it's my



assumption that


Starbucks
would
miss
out on even
more
sales than
with two



options.





The Grande is a
decoy, and it
makes
all
the difference.
Who


knew coffee
was
such a
great

wingman?

What's the

impact?

That's
right.

Simply
by

giving


multiple



choices

clear



winner,

way more



people
will

buy

your

products.
